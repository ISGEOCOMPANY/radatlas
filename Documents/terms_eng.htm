Terms and abbreviations

Activity [6]

Value defined as the number of spontaneous nuclear transformations dN in the time interval dt:

A = dN/dt.

Unit - Becquerel (Bq).

Accident [6]

~ radiation

any unplanned event at any object with radiation or radiation-nuclear technology if the following two necessary and sufficient conditions are observed:

- loss of control over radiation source;

- actual (or potential) exposure of people to radiation due to loss of control over radiation source.

~ Off-site

Radiation accident with consequences that are not limited by the premises of the object and its site but are spread to adjacent territories where lives population that can actually or potentially be exposed.

~ Global

Off-site radiation accident impacting a majority or complete territory of the country and its population.

~ Transboundary

Global radiation accident when accident zone is spreading across the state boundary of the country where it occurred.

Nuclear Power Plant (NPP) [6]

nuclear plant aimed at electricity generation.

Dose [6]

~ equivalent in organ or tissue T (HT)

measure defined as multiplication of absorbed dose DT to the separate organ or tissue T with the radiation weighting factor wR:

HT = DT*wR .

equivalent dose unit in system SI is Sievert (Sv)

~ effective (E)

sum of products of equivalent doses HT in separate organs or tissues with weighting factor wT:

E=SS HT*wT.

Utilization of effective dose concept is accepted if equivalent dose values are lower than a threshold for occurrence of deterministic effects.

~ absorbed (D)

measure of the average amount of energy dе from an ionizing radiation deposited in the voluentary unit to mass dm of some materials in this volume:

D = dе/dm

Unit of measurement in the system Si is gray (Gy)

~ reference

effective dose calculated according to methodological approaches regulated by document [4]. This dose is weight-averaged by professional and age structure of in habitants of settlements (separately for rural and urban population).

Acceptable level [6]

derivative unit for radionuclide intake to human body per calendar year, average annual equivalent dose rate, radionuclide concentration in air, potable water and ration, particle flux density etc. calculated for reference irradiation conditions from dose limits values.

Controlled zone [6]

territory with enhanced radioecological and dosimetric monitoring.

Passport dose components [5]

Calculated by standardized methodologies annual effective doses of external irradiation, internal irradiation from alimentary intake of cesium and strontium radioisotopes, irradiation from all intake routes of plutonium and other transuranium element of Chornobyl origin. Irradiation from industrial sources is also included to passport dose components.

Passport dose of settlement [5]

Average effective dose that potentially can be received by inhabitant of a given settlement from all radiation sources of Chornobyl and industrial origins during current year. Exposure of thyroid gland by iodine radioisotopes occurred in 1986 is not included in the components of the passport dose of settlement.

Specific activity

activity per unit weight (mass specific activity, Аm), volume (volumetric specific activity, АV) or surface (surface specific activity, АS):

Аm=А/M, АV=А/V, АS=А/S,

where А- activity; M - mass; V - volume; S - surface.

Measurement units respectively – Becquerel per kilogram (Bq/kg-1), Becquerel per cubic meter -3) or Becquerel per square meter (Bq/m -2).

Irradiation [7]

impact on a human being of ionizing radiation from sources outside from human body (external irradiation) or from sources inside of human body (internal irradiation).

Radionuclides [6]

radioactive atoms with given mass number and atomic number. Radionuclides of the same chemical element are called its radioactive isotopes.

Radiation influence [6]

any type of radiation influence that cause or may cause irradiation of human being of radioactive contamination of environment.

Radioactivity [6]

spontaneous transformation of atomic nuclei to nuclei of other elements. Accompanied by ionizing radiation. There are four types of radioactivity: alpha decay, beta decay, spontaneous fission of atomic nuclei, proton radioactivity.

Radioactive contamination [6]

presence or spread of radioactive substances exceeding its natural content in environment and/or human body.

Thyreodosimetric passportization [5]

Standardized system for retrospective reconstruction of thyroid gland irradiation dose by radioiodine of Chornobyl radioactive discharge aimed at getting thyreodosimetric passport of the settlement

Contamination density (specific surface activity) [6]

activity of given radionuclide і on given surface Р with area S:

s = А/S.

Measurement unit – Becquerel per square meter (Bq/m -2).

RBMK

reactor bolshoy moshchnosty kanalny - high-power channel reactor

Chornobyl NPP

Chornobyl nuclear power plan
